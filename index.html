portada-engine  ·  22 Live Markets

A config-driven multi-tenant civic news platform.

portada-engine is a config-driven multi-tenant political news aggregation platform I conceived, designed, and shipped. Twenty-two live markets in production, all serving from a single GitHub repository as one unified Vercel project with host-based routing. Three brand families: La Portada (Spanish, Latin America and Spain), The StatePage (English, US, with state subdomains under a national apex), and The EnglandPage (English, country-level standalone, introducing a wordmark schema pattern reusable for future country sites).

As product owner, creative director, and technical director, I made every product, editorial, design, and architectural decision. I directed the refactor from a single-market codebase into a config-driven engine, and later directed the Phase B/D/E collapse of eleven per-market Vercel projects into one unified host-routed project. I wrote the editorial prompt architecture for each market, decomposed into 11 reusable per-market components (relevance rules, category routing, tie-breakers, KEEP/DROP examples, importance scoring, briefing voice) with anti-inflation discipline in shared engine code. AI coding assistants generated all production code under my direction.

All twenty-two markets run shared infrastructure (Next.js 14 App Router, Vercel Pro, Cloudflare DNS and Registrar, region-split Google Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite keys for editorial briefing generation, dual-layer cache with fresh and backup tiers, dual-typeface system) adapted at every layer to each market through configuration. Categorization is rule-based with zero AI calls. The Social Pulse module (Bluesky, YouTube RSS, generic RSS) handles non-news signal at zero AI cost.

AI cost engineering. Drove monthly Gemini spend from roughly $300 in a few days on broken calls to roughly $1.41/month at ten markets on hourly cadence through architectural decisions. Moved categorization from AI to deterministic rules, decoupled the briefing cache, region-split API keys for independent spend caps, benchmarked three models before committing. Projected under $10/month at twenty-two markets.

AI governance in production. When a briefing factual-drift failure surfaced on one market, directed a shared-engine fix (factual-constraint guardrail, twenty-story input cap, length recalibration) that protected all twenty-two markets in one deploy rather than rewriting twenty-two per-market prompts. Added categorization provenance tracking and a five-tier lead-picker fence so unmatched or off-topic headlines never win the homepage lead. 649 Vitest tests cover host-map resolution, the categorizer, the lead-picker, the RSS pipeline, palette validation, deprecated-field guards, Social Pulse filtering, the voices-route language guard, and per-market keyword coverage.
